Personal Projects

The American Psyche - internally known as Twitter Trend Sentiment utilized Twitter's Trending Topics API and Google's Natural Language Processing API to determine the sentiment of a trending topic. Users could search for major cities and observe whether or not the summation of all teh city's trending topics resulted in a positive, neutral, or sad/angry attitude. This project was developed at Google's CSSI in 2019 in collaboration with Ethan Buranday and Jason Li.

Serv gave any business or group that queues customers easy cacess to basic business metrics as well as customer management. Groups that partnered with Serv would receive an NFC tag that patrons would activate with their phone and enter their contact infomration into upon being redirected to our website, allowing the user to continue their day elsewhere while waiting for their appointment or reservation. Once the business had an opening for the patron, they would receive a text informing the patron to return to the business soon, or their spot would be forfeited. This project was developed at NWHacks 2020 in collaboration with Johnny Li, Byeongjun Moon, Maya Evans, and Frank Whoee.
