Having grown up in the suburbs of Seattle, I've been lucky enough to experience the lush environments of the Cascade mountain range. My participation in the Boy Scouts of America also gave me plenty of exposure to wildlife, and as such I enjoy taking pictures of trees and mountains. Nature provides an excellent break from technology, and while it seems ironic that I use technology to capture the moment I like to take a little piece of nature back home with me after a trip.

While they don't always make for the best picture, any time where there's open water with the sun or the moon I race for my camera. I live near a popular park that sits on the Puget Sound, so photos of the Sound dominate my photo gallery. I have a lot of fond memories at this park, and the nearby boat launch area features a kayak rental shop. The area around the park sees a lot of investment today, with hotels and residencies opening up to increase interest in the area, and given the views available I think that interest and investment is warranted.

Monuments and cultural structures always capture my attention. The history and effort put into each building once made me want to pursue architecture, but seeing as my artistic skills aren't exactly the best I decided to keep my admiration of architecture while moving onto other career prospects. I took this picture while I was in Korea, and this mini-temple sits on a backroad within Seoul. Strangely, on the other side of the street are apartment complexes and just down the road is a bustling night market, so this structure doesn't really fit anywhere.

Ever since night-photography became so easy with smartphones, I snap a picture whenever there's something worthy of a picture, often resulting in a cool effect where the prominent color in frame blows up and floods the photo. UW's quad is seen here, where instead of the iconic cherry blossoms during Spring the glaze on the brick pathways create a cool reflection of the nearby lights while the snow contrasts the dark aspect of the image. My only disappointment is that the left part of the image with the trees gets blown out because there either wasn't enough light or the exposure wasn't long enough.
